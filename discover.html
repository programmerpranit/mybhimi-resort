PLACES TO VISIT

JUNNAR DARSHAN

Junnar is a historically famous place in Maharashtra which happens to be the birthplace of the great warrior king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is just 14 km far from Mybhumi resort.

SHIVNERI

Shivneri Fort is a 17th-century military fortification located 14 km from Mybhumi agro resort. It is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj , the emperor and founder of Maratha Empire. The fort extends up to 1 mi (1.6 km) with seven spiral well-defended gates. There are mud walls all around the fort. Inside the fort, the major buildings are the prayer hall, a tomb and a mosque.

VIGHNAHAR OZAR

The Vigneshwara Temple or Vighnahar Ganapati Temple of Ozar is a Hindu temple dedicated to Ganesha, the elephant-headed god of wisdom. The temple is one of the Ashtavinayaka, the eight revered shrines of Ganesha is around 10 km from Mybhumi agro resort.

MALSHEJ GHAT

Malshej Ghat is a mountain pass in the Western Ghats range in the Kalyan-Ahmednagar Road about 28 km from Us. The site is nestled in the lofty rugged hills of the Western Ghats.

NANEGHAT

Nane Ghat, is a mountain pass in the Western Ghats range between the Konkan coast and the ancient town of Junnar in the Deccan plateau around 41 km from Mybhumi agro tourism

GMRT

The Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope (GMRT), is an array of thirty fully steerable parabolic radio telescopes of 45 metre diameter, observing at metre wavelengths. It is operated by the National Centre for Radio Astrophysics. At the time it was built, it was the world's largest interferometric array offering a baseline of upto 25 kilometres. It is just 33 km far from Mybhumi resort.

CHAVAND

Chavand is a hill fortress in Junnar district. The fort, which rises 1,036 meters (3,399 feet) above sea level, is located in the Sahyadri mountain range. It is just 28 km far from Mybhumi resort.

LENYADRI

Lenyadri, sometimes called Ganesa Lena, Ganesh Pahar Caves, are a series of about 30 rock-cut Buddhist caves, located about 4 kilometres from us.

JIVDHAN

Jivdhan (or Jeevdhan) is a hill fortress situated 1 km near the modern day town of Ghatghar located in the Sahyadri mountain range. Jivdhan is a part of the 'Famous 5' trekking destinations among trekking enthusiasts. This is a high difficulty grade trek due to the misleading jungle tracks and the necessity and knowledge of using climbing equipment.
